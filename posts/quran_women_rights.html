Comparison of Women’s Rights

Qur’an vs. Vedas & Smriti Texts

Category Qur’an Vedas (Early Texts) Smriti Texts (Later Texts)
Inheritance Son gets double the share of daughter (4:11) Not explicitly restricted Daughters inherit less or are subordinate (Manusmriti)
Marriage & Authority Men as protectors; polygyny allowed (4:3, 4:34) Women honored in marriage hymns (Rigveda 10.85) Women subordinate to father → husband → sons
Education Encourages knowledge for all; no explicit ban Women like Gargi, Maitreyi studied and debated philosophy Women forbidden from Vedic study (Manusmriti 9.18)
Divorce Men have easier divorce (ṭalāq); women can seek khulʿ Not codified Widow remarriage discouraged
Witness/Testimony Two women = one man in financial testimony (2:282) Not systematized Male-centered legal authority
Polygamy Allowed up to 4 wives if justice is maintained (4:3) Not mentioned Sometimes allowed for men

Contextual Insights

  • Qur’an (7th Century Arabia): Partial reform in a patriarchal society; improved women’s rights but maintained inequality.
  • Vedas (1500–500 BCE): Women participated in rituals and philosophy; no explicit gender inequality laws.
  • Smriti Texts (200 BCE–6th Century CE): Codified patriarchal norms, restricting women’s inheritance, education, and autonomy.

Key Takeaways

  • Qur’an: Improved women’s rights for its time but reflects patriarchal norms.
  • Vedas: Early texts respected women’s roles; later Smriti texts imposed restrictions.
  • Smriti Texts: Introduced stricter patriarchal rules absent in the Vedas.

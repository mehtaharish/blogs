ਸਲੋਕੁ ਮਃ ੧ ॥ Salok, 1st Mehl (by Guru Nanak Dev Ji)

ਦਇਆ ਕਪਾਹ ਸੰਤੋਖੁ ਸੂਤੁ ਜਤੁ ਗੰਢੀ ਸਤੁ ਵਟੁ ॥
Compassion is the cotton, contentment is the thread, self-restraint is the knot, and truth is the twist of the sacred thread.

ਏਹੁ ਜਨੇਊ ਜੀਅ ਕਾ ਹਈ ਤ ਪਾਡੇ ਘਤੁ ॥੧॥
This is the sacred thread of the soul; wearing this, all sins are cleansed.

ਨਾ ਏਹੁ ਤੁਟੈ ਨ ਮਲੁ ਲਗੈ ਨਾ ਏਹੁ ਜਲੈ ਨ ਜਾਇ ॥
It neither breaks, nor gets soiled, nor burns; it is eternal.

ਧੰਨੁ ਸੁ ਮਾਣਸ ਨਾਨਕਾ ਜੋ ਗਲਿ ਚਲੇ ਪਾਇ ॥
Blessed is that person, says Nanak, who wears this sacred thread around the neck of the soul (lives by these virtues).

ਚਉਕੜਿ ਮੁਲਿ ਅਣਾਇਆ ਬਹਿ ਚਉਕੈ ਪਾਇਆ ॥
Even if someone wears an external sacred thread, its material value is meaningless without virtues.

ਸਿਖਾ ਕੰਨਿ ਚੜਾਈਆ ਗੁਰੁ ਬ੍ਰਾਹਮਣੁ ਥਿਆ ॥
Merely following rituals like ear-piercing or wearing the janeu does not make one a true Sikh or Brahmin.

ਓਹੁ ਮੁਆ ਓਹੁ ਝੜਿ ਪਇਆ ਵੇਤਗਾ ਗਇਆ ॥ ੧ ॥
Those who focus only on outward rituals are spiritually dead; their external thread is useless. (1)

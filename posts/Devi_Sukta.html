Meaning of Devi

Devi in Rigveda 10.125 (Devi Sukta / Vak Sukta) has a deeper meaning. Here, ‚ÄúDevi‚Äù is not a specific worshipped form (like Durga, Lakshmi, or Saraswati). Here, Devi means:

1. Vak (Speech / Word / Logos)

  • This Sukta was composed by a female Rishika ‚Äì Ambhrini (the speaker), who presented herself as Vak (speech, voice).
  • Vak here is not just ‚Äúspoken speech,‚Äù but the divine voice that gives birth to creation.
  • In this sense, it is similar to the Logos (word-principle) that governs everything. Just as the Bible says, ‚ÄúIn the beginning was the Word (Logos)‚Ä¶,‚Äù the Vedas say: the word is the foundation of creation.
  • Here, Vak is considered a cosmic sound-energy that drives creation.

2. Shakti (Cosmic Energy / Universal Power)

  • Devi here is a universal creative power that empowers all deities.
  • Indra, Agni, Varuna ‚Äî all derive their strength from Devi.
  • It is important to understand that the Vedic vision is: Devi is the root of nature and energy.

3. Manifestation of Brahman

  • This Sukta says, ‚ÄúI am everywhere, I support heaven and earth.‚Äù
  • Here, Devi = the manifested form of Brahman (the ultimate reality).
  • Later, in the Puranas, this was referred to as ‚ÄúMaha-Maya,‚Äù ‚ÄúJagat-Janani,‚Äù or ‚ÄúDurga.‚Äù

4. Source of Kingship, Wealth, and Knowledge

  • Devi says that she can make anyone a priest, king, poet, or scholar as she desires.
  • This means that this power is the inherent principle behind all social and religious order.

üåü Simple Understanding

  • Devi here = the all-pervading primordial feminine power that governs everything.
  • She is the mother of creation,
  • She gives strength to all gods and humans,
  • And she pervades everything as Vak (word, knowledge, consciousness).
  • Later, she was established as Devi Saraswati, Ambika, Chandi, and Durga.

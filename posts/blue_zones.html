Blue Zones

Blue Zones are places where an unusually high number of people live long, healthy lives. The five best-known Blue Zones are:

  • Okinawa, Japan
  • Sardinia, Italy
  • Ikaria, Greece
  • Nicoya Peninsula, Costa Rica
  • Loma Linda, California, USA

There is no single secret that makes people live longer. Their food, movement, relationships and general way of life may all contribute.

Common habits of people in Blue Zones

1 They move naturally

Most do not follow hard gym programmes. Instead, they remain active during daily life by:

  • Walking
  • Gardening
  • Cooking
  • Cleaning
  • Climbing stairs
  • Doing physical work

Their bodies move regularly throughout the day.

2 They eat mainly plant-based food

Their traditional meals usually include:

  • Beans and lentils
  • Vegetables
  • Whole grains
  • Potatoes and sweet potatoes
  • Fruits and nuts

They generally eat relatively little meat, sugar and highly processed food.

3 They avoid overeating

In Okinawa, people traditionally say "Hara Hachi Bu." It means stopping eating when you feel around 80% full.

They often eat slowly and have smaller portions.

4 They have a purpose in life

They have a reason to get up in the morning. This could be:

  • Looking after their family
  • Gardening
  • Helping neighbours
  • Working
  • Practising a skill
  • Taking care of animals

Having a purpose may support mental and physical well-being.

5 They have close relationships

Family, friends and neighbours are important. Older people are less likely to be socially isolated.

In Okinawa, a lifelong group of supportive friends is sometimes called a moai.

6 They regularly reduce stress

They may relax through:

  • Prayer or meditation
  • Afternoon naps
  • Quiet time
  • Meeting friends
  • Spending time outside
  • Slowing down during meals

They also experience stress, but relaxation is a normal part of their day.

7 Their environment supports healthy habits

Walking, fresh food and social contact are part of normal life. Healthy choices happen naturally instead of depending completely on discipline or willpower.

A simple Blue-Zone-style day

Walk regularly  →  eat beans, vegetables and whole foods  →  avoid overeating  →  spend time with family or friends  →  do something meaningful  →  relax  →  sleep properly.

Some descriptions of Blue Zones include moderate wine consumption. However, drinking alcohol is not necessary for a long life. The World Health Organization states that alcohol carries health risks, including an increased risk of cancer.

Read the source: WHO Europe – No level of alcohol consumption is safe for our health

Blue Zones do not prove that following these habits will make everyone live to 100. Researchers also debate some claims and historical age records. Nevertheless, regular movement, nutritious plant-rich food, good relationships, adequate rest and avoiding smoking are sensible habits for a healthier life.
